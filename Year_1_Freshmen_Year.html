Classes to Take

It is important to have a general 2 year plan at Pierce, so start talking with advisors at both Pierce and UW Tacoma. When you start registering for classes, you should have already been paired with a general advisor who would have showed you the Associates of Science Track 2, which requires a lot of math and physics. As of today, you do not need that much math or physics, but instead, you will need the following courses:

  • English 101
  • Composition English class
  • Math 141 (Pre-Calculus 1)
  • Math 142 (Pre-Calculus 2)
  • Math 151 (Calculus 1)
  • Math 152 (Calculus 2)
  • Math 146 (Statistics)
  • CS 142 (Introduction to Programming with Python)
  • CS 202 (Intermediate Object Oriented Programming with Java)

In my opinion, the best way to get prepared for UW-Tacoma's Computer Science program is by not only taking these classes, but also getting a general Associates of Arts degree alongside these pre-requisites.

A suggested 4 year plan can be found on the following URL: UW Tacoma's Plan

I recommend that you begin talking to the advisors from your target school ASAP. Here are the emails of the adviors from the Institute of Technology, which is in charge of the Computer Science curriculum: Advising Team Computer Science

Equivalency Guides

The transfer policy's at any unviersity can be a bit tricky to navigate, so I'll just post the UW - Tacoma's equivalency guide here and give a few examples of how to use it. Pierce To UW Equivalency Guide So to use this guy, let's assume that we wanted to see how Calculus 1, or Math 151 in Pierce Terms, would transfer to UW's system.

  1. Select "Mathematics" hyper link
  2. Search for "Math& 151"
  3. Look at the corresponding course on the right
As we have learned, Math 151 will transfer over to the University of Washington as Math 124.

WARNING: As time continues, certain classes may or may not be on the equivalency guide, make sure to double check with the advisors at UW to see how the credits will transfer. This further proves my initial point of the importance of talking to the UW advisors ASAP.

And there you go, you now know how to use the Pierce - UW equivalency guide.
